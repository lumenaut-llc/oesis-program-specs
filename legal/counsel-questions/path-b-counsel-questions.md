# Path B Counsel Questions

## Purpose

Give outside counsel or a limited legal review a short, high-signal question set tied to the April 14, 2026 preview and the intended later open release.

## Patent counsel

Questions:

- Is the current narrow invention candidate specific enough to justify a provisional before April 14?
- Which withheld materials most need to be included for adequate written-description support?
- Are any planned social posts, diagrams, or preview docs already enabling enough to create avoidable disclosure risk?
- If the provisional is filed, which preview materials are safe to release immediately afterward?
- Are there obvious inventorship or ownership issues to resolve before filing?

Why it matters:

- the project wants only a short delay before open release, so the filing has to be narrow, real, and timed correctly

## Open-source counsel

Questions:

- Is the intended later split among AGPLv3, CERN-OHL-S v2, CC BY-SA 4.0, and possible ODbL use coherent for this repo?
- Should firmware follow the software copyleft license or a different license?
- Should the project adopt a DCO first, or is a CLA warranted for patent-risk or governance reasons?
- What should `IP.md` say now without accidentally overpromising a future patent non-assert or patent grant?

Why it matters:

- the April 14 preview will publicly signal the licensing direction before the final technical release

## Privacy and data counsel

Questions:

- Do the current parcel operator-ownership and private-by-default statements overpromise relative to what the software can actually support in MVP form?
- Are the sharing modes clear enough to avoid implying anonymization or public-data rights where they do not exist?
- What additional disclosures are needed before any real pilot or neighborhood-sharing deployment?

Why it matters:

- the project’s trust posture depends on the gap between policy language and actual controls staying small

## Product-liability or general counsel

Questions:

- Are the claims, limitations, and safety-language rules strong enough for a public preview?
- Which user-facing labels or example outputs create avoidable risk if shown publicly?
- What extra warning language is needed before any field deployment or pilot recruitment?

Why it matters:

- parcel-level status language can drift into implied safety or emergency guidance if not checked early

## Internal prep before sending to counsel

Attach:

- public-preview-scope.md
- holdback-list.md
- provisional-packet-outline.md
- current claims and safety-language standard
- one-page summary of the chosen narrow invention candidate
