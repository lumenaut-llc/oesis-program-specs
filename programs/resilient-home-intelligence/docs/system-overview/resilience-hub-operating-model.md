# Resilience-Hub Operating Model

## Purpose

Define how shared community spaces such as schools, churches, libraries, and community centers can participate as resilience hubs inside the broader network.

## Why resilience hubs matter

A neighborhood resilience system becomes more useful when it can identify not only stressed homes, but also trusted community places that may serve as:

- cooling centers
- clean-air spaces
- charging and communications points
- information and coordination anchors

## Core role of a hub

A resilience hub is not just another participant node.
It is a shared community asset that may need to expose more public readiness information than a private home while still preserving appropriate operational boundaries.

## Hub-specific outputs

- hub readiness
- air quality readiness
- cooling and heating readiness
- power and communications readiness
- occupancy or capacity posture later, if explicitly supported

## Participation model

### Hub private operations layer

Internal conditions and operational data used by hub stewards.

### Hub shared neighborhood layer

Derived readiness signals visible to nearby participants.

### Public or partner layer later

More bounded public-facing hub status if governance allows.

## Example use cases

- identify whether a community center appears ready to serve as a cooling refuge
- show whether a church or library has power and acceptable indoor air
- help residents compare household stress with hub readiness

## Hub operator role

Likely capabilities:

- update hub operating status
- review hub-specific readiness
- publish or confirm shared hub availability

The product should separate:

- automated sensed readiness
- human-confirmed operational availability

## Important distinction

A sensed "ready" state is not the same thing as a hub officially opening to the public.

The system should distinguish:

- physical readiness indicators
- confirmed operational status

## Risks to avoid

- implying a hub is open when no human has confirmed it
- treating occupancy or vulnerable-population data casually
- publishing more building detail than necessary

## Open questions

- What minimum signals should a hub share to be useful?
- When should the system require human confirmation before showing hub availability?
