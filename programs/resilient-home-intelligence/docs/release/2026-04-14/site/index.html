Homeowner-owned environmental sensing, built parcel first.

Resilient Home Intelligence is a modular, homeowner-centered effort to build parcel-level environmental awareness for smoke, flooding and runoff, and heat without forcing homeowners to surrender raw data to a centralized platform.

Parcel first

The project aims to generate parcel-level condition estimates instead of relying only on coarse regional signals.

Homeowner controlled

Raw homeowner-contributed data is intended to remain homeowner-controlled rather than automatically becoming public or platform-owned.

Modular and standalone

The system is designed so modular builds can work independently and still interoperate as part of a broader network.

Honest about uncertainty

Outputs are meant to carry confidence, freshness, and evidence-mode information rather than pretending every result is equally certain.

Governance and privacy highlights

Data ownership

Homeowners should own the raw data generated by their parcel deployments. Platform operation does not imply a blanket transfer of ownership.

Sharing posture

Exact parcel-linked raw data should remain private unless a defined opt-in sharing mode permits something broader.

Claims boundary

The project may provide parcel-level condition estimates and evidence summaries. It must not present itself as emergency authority or a safety certifier.

Release scope

The public preview is intentionally non-enabling while the project preserves its current release sequencing and filing options.

Important limitations

  • Outputs are generated from available evidence, not direct certainty.
  • Conditions may change faster than the system updates.
  • Missing or stale inputs reduce confidence.
  • The project does not claim full neighborhood visibility.
