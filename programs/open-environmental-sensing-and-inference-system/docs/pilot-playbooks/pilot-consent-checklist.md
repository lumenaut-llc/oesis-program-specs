# Pilot Consent Checklist

## Purpose

Provide a practical checklist for onboarding pilot participants without drifting away from the private-by-default and opt-in sharing model.

## Before enrollment

- explain the pilot purpose in plain language
- explain which hazards are in scope for the pilot
- explain that outputs are condition estimates, not emergency instructions or guarantees
- explain the default private-only posture
- explain any optional sharing modes separately

## Sharing explanation

For each optional sharing mode offered in the pilot, confirm that the participant was told:
- what data leaves the private parcel context
- whether the outgoing data is delayed, derived, or aggregated
- who may see it
- what it is used for
- how to revoke it
- what happens to previously aggregated outputs after revocation

## Participant controls

- show where sharing settings live in the product
- show how to request export
- show how to request deletion
- show who to contact for questions or complaints

## Recordkeeping

- record the notice version shown
- record the sharing modes enabled
- record the time consent was given
- record any revocation later made during the pilot

## Red lines

- do not bundle neighborhood or research sharing into basic pilot participation if private-only use is feasible
- do not use vague phrases like improve the community without concrete explanation
- do not imply that joining the pilot makes the participant's parcel publicly visible
