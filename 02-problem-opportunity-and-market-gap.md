# Problem, opportunity, and market gap

**Canonical incorporation:** Problem and opportunity summary → [`architecture/system/vision-and-use-cases.md`](architecture/system/vision-and-use-cases.md) (and mission in [`program/README.md`](program/README.md)). This file keeps the full argument.

## Current problem

People can access public hazard information and buy isolated sensors, but they still do not have a trustworthy system that answers:

- What is likely true for this property right now?
- How sure are we?
- What evidence supports that conclusion?
- What does that mean for staying, leaving, reentering, or protecting assets?

The current gap is a mismatch between the scale of available information and the scale of the real decision.

## Longer-term problem

The deeper long-term problem is not just missing hyperlocal data.

It is the lack of a durable local adaptation evidence base:
- how hazards show up at the parcel level
- how buildings and routes respond
- what interventions help
- what outcomes changed
- where neighborhood weak points actually are

## Opportunity

The project’s opportunity is to become the missing layer between:
- coarse public hazard systems
- isolated consumer sensors and smart-home products
- household operational decisions
- neighborhood and community resilience planning

## Market gap

There is currently no strong parcel-first, multi-hazard, private-by-default intelligence layer that bridges public hazard systems and isolated consumer devices while remaining useful under partial adoption.

## Strong long-term benefit framing

The strongest long-term benefit is that this becomes a locally grounded climate and disaster intelligence layer.

It can improve recency, parcel relevance, and long-run adaptation learning without requiring homeowners to surrender raw data ownership.

## Value proposition by scale

### For households

The value is not just more data.
It is better interpretation.

### For neighborhoods

The value is partial-adoption intelligence.
One household can benefit alone, but additional participating homes can improve nearby coverage, confidence, and inference quality.

### For the long term

The value is a new local evidence layer for climate adaptation, preparedness, and resilience planning.

### For the market

Public systems are usually regional and hazard-specific.
Consumer devices are usually local but not parcel-intelligent.
This system sits in the middle: multi-hazard, parcel-first, owner-controlled, inference-driven, and designed to improve with shared evidence rather than replace official systems.
