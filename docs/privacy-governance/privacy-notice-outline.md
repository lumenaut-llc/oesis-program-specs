# Privacy Notice Outline

## Purpose

Provide the structure for a user-facing privacy notice that reflects the MVP's actual data handling model.

## Suggested sections

### 1. What the service does

- explain that the system provides parcel-level environmental condition estimates based on available evidence
- avoid describing the product as a safety certifier or emergency authority

### 2. Data categories

- private parcel data
- shared data
- public context
- derived parcel states
- administrative and governance records

### 3. How data is used

- operate the parcel product
- generate parcel-level estimates
- support user-selected sharing modes
- support troubleshooting, calibration, security, and audit

### 4. Sharing choices

- private-only baseline
- network assist if offered
- neighborhood aggregate contribution if offered
- research or pilot participation if offered

### 5. What is not shared by default

- exact parcel location in public or neighborhood views
- raw parcel observations across households
- occupancy-adjacent or household-sensitive inferences

### 6. Public and third-party sources

- identify that weather, flood, smoke, terrain, and similar context may come from public or third-party sources
- explain that these sources have their own update cadence and limitations

### 7. Provenance and uncertainty

- explain that outputs are estimates based on available evidence
- explain that stale or missing inputs reduce confidence

### 8. User rights and controls

- access
- export
- delete
- revoke sharing
- review current sharing settings

### 9. Retention

- summarize category-based retention at a high level

### 10. Security and incidents

- explain that parcel-linked data is treated as sensitive and subject to security and access controls

## Drafting rule

The final privacy notice should describe actual product behavior, not aspirational future architecture.
