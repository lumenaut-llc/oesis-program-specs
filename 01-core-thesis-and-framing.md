## Core thesis

This project should be framed as a parcel-first, multi-scale resilience intelligence system.

It starts at the household scale, but its long-term purpose is broader:
to create the best possible information layer for disaster preparedness, disaster relief, and infrastructure climate resilience by combining local observations, shared neighborhood evidence, parcel context, route context, and later community lifeline context.

The parcel is the anchor.
It is not the final boundary of the system.

## What the system is

At baseline, this is not just a sensor kit and not just a dashboard.

It is a system that:
- gathers direct observations
- fuses them with parcel priors and public context
- estimates current local conditions
- shows confidence, evidence source, and uncertainty
- stays useful under partial adoption
- preserves homeowner ownership and private-by-default data handling

Long term, it should evolve from:

sensing → inference → functional interpretation → intervention and verification → route, block, and lifeline resilience intelligence

## Best one-sentence description

This is a parcel-first, multi-scale resilience intelligence system that starts with household-level sensing and inference, then expands into route, block, and community lifeline intelligence while preserving private-by-default homeowner data ownership.

## Strongest long-term target

The best target is not more local sensor data.

## Best wording to use

Use:
- parcel-first, multi-scale
- evidence-based
- uncertainty-aware
- private by default
- shared by choice
- useful under partial adoption
- complements official systems
- route, block, and lifeline expansion over time

Avoid:
- more accurate than all official systems
- regulatory-grade by default
- autonomous disaster authority
- already a full neighborhood resilience platform
- fully solved adaptation engine
