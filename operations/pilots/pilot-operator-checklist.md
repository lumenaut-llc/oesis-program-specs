# Pilot Operator Checklist

## Purpose

Provide a minimum operator checklist for running a first pilot block without ad hoc handling of privacy, claims, or access decisions.

## Before pilot launch

- confirm the active pilot purpose and scope
- confirm the enrolled sharing modes and default posture
- confirm the notice and consent flow is ready
- confirm operator access rules are documented
- confirm no public parcel-by-parcel map will be created from pilot data
- confirm data publication boundaries are documented

## Technical readiness

- confirm device inventory and parcel assignments are documented
- confirm uptime and data freshness monitoring exists
- confirm stale-data behavior is visible in the product
- confirm export and deletion requests have an intake path
- confirm access to parcel-linked data is logged

## Communications readiness

- define the participant support contact
- define the incident escalation contact
- define who can approve pilot result publication
- define approved language for participant-facing updates

## During pilot operation

- monitor for stale or missing evidence that could be misread as certainty
- monitor whether shared-map thresholds are suppressing low-participation outputs as intended
- log complaints, revocations, export requests, and deletion requests
- avoid informal data pulls outside approved workflows

## Before publishing findings

- remove exact parcel-linked contributed data from public outputs
- verify no map, chart, or table singles out one household
- distinguish public context from participant-contributed evidence
- avoid implying broader coverage or accuracy than the pilot actually achieved
